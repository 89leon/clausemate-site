PRIVACY POLICY

Nothing leaves
this phone.

Effective April 25, 2026 · Version 1.0

The short version

Clausemate collects nothing about you, stores nothing on any server, and sends nothing off your device. We don't have a database because we don't have anything to put in one.

What the app does with your data

When you scan a contract, the images and recognized text stay in your iPhone's local storage. Optical character recognition, clause classification, plain-language summaries, red-flag detection, and every answer in the Ask tab are all generated on-device using Apple's VisionKit text engine and Clausemate's local processing code. None of it transits the internet.

The "0 bytes sent" counter in the Profile tab is live, not cosmetic. It reflects what Clausemate has actually sent over the network since launch. It stays at zero because the app makes no outbound network calls.

No accounts, no identifiers

You don't sign up. You don't sign in. We don't assign you a user ID, device fingerprint, install ID, or any other token we could tie to you later. There is no analytics SDK, no crash reporter that phones home, no third-party tracker.

Optional on-device language model

If you choose to download the optional multilingual language model from within the app (approximately 3 GB), that download fetches a model file from Apple's servers. Apple may log the download itself according to its own policies. After it's on your device, the model runs locally like everything else. You can skip this download; the app works fully in English without it.

Purchases

If you tip Clausemate using the optional Pro options (one-time "Thanks tip", lifetime "Pro supporter", or monthly "Monthly supporter"), Apple processes the payment through the App Store. We never see your payment method, name, or email. Apple's Privacy Policy governs that transaction. Clausemate receives from Apple only an anonymous transaction identifier confirming the purchase, used to grant the supporter badge locally.

Camera and photo library

Clausemate asks for camera access only to scan printed contracts. It asks for photo library access only if you choose to import an existing photo of a contract. Neither permission is used for anything else. If you deny both, the app still works on sample text.

Deleting your data

The Profile tab's "Delete everything" button removes every document, clause, summary, and Ask entry from this iPhone's local storage. There is nothing elsewhere to delete, because nothing exists elsewhere.

Children

Clausemate is not designed for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any information from anyone — including children — because we do not collect information at all.

Changes to this policy

If Clausemate ever changes this policy — for example, if a future version adds an optional feature that requires network access — we will update the effective date above and describe the change clearly in the app's release notes. Past privacy cannot be retroactively revoked.

Contact

Questions about this policy? Email 89leon@gmail.com. Please allow a few business days for a reply.

This policy describes the behavior of Clausemate for iPhone, version 1.0 and later, distributed through Apple's App Store.
