1. What Clausemate is

Clausemate is an iPhone app that helps you read contracts. You scan or import a document, and Clausemate produces a plain-language summary plus per-clause notes. It is not a law firm. Using it does not create an attorney-client relationship, and nothing it shows you is legal advice tailored to your situation. For decisions that materially affect you, consult a licensed attorney.

2. Eligibility

You must be at least 13 to use Clausemate. The app isn't designed for younger users. If your jurisdiction requires a higher age, follow that.

3. No account, no terms about accounts

Clausemate doesn't have user accounts. There's nothing for you to maintain, sign in to, or lose access to. Everything happens on your iPhone.

4. Acceptable use

Use Clausemate to read contracts you have a legitimate reason to read. Don't reverse-engineer the app, scrape content from it, or use it to commit fraud. Beyond that, we trust you.

5. AI limitations

Clausemate's analysis is generated from hand-authored templates and, in v1.1+, on-device language models. It can be wrong. It can miss things. It can describe a clause incorrectly. Before you act on anything Clausemate tells you, verify it with the actual contract and a licensed attorney.

• A one-time "Thanks tip" ($4.99 USD or local equivalent) — non-consumable.
• A lifetime "Pro supporter" ($14.99) — non-consumable, family-shared.
• A monthly "Monthly supporter" ($4.99/month) — auto-renewing subscription.

All payments are processed by Apple through the App Store. Apple's terms govern the transaction itself. We never see your card or billing details.

Subscriptions auto-renew until cancelled. Cancel anytime through Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions on your iPhone. Refunds are handled by Apple at reportaproblem.apple.com.
